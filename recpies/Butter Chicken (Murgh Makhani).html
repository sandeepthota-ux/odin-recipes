Butter Chicken (Murgh Makhani)

Description:

Butter Chicken, also known as Murgh Makhani, is a creamy and rich Indian dish that originated in Delhi. It's made with marinated chicken cooked in a velvety tomato-based gravy enriched with butter and cream. The flavors are aromatic with a hint of sweetness, making it a favorite among many. The dish is typically paired with naan (Indian flatbread) or steamed basmati rice.

Ingredients:

  • Chicken (boneless, cut into pieces) – 500g
  • Butter – 3 tbsp
  • Onions – 1 large (chopped)
  • Tomatoes – 2 large (pureed)
  • Ginger-garlic paste – 1 tbsp
  • Cream – 1/4 cup
  • Garam masala – 1 tsp
  • Chili powder – 1 tsp
  • Turmeric powder – 1/2 tsp
  • Kasuri methi (dried fenugreek leaves) – 1 tsp
  • Salt – to taste
  • Fresh cilantro – for garnish

Steps:

  1. Marinate the chicken with yogurt, chili powder, turmeric, garam masala, and salt for at least 30 minutes.
  2. Heat butter in a pan and sauté onions and ginger-garlic paste until golden brown.
  3. Add pureed tomatoes and cook until the oil separates.
  4. Add the marinated chicken and cook until it turns white and tender.
  5. Stir in cream and kasuri methi, and cook for a few more minutes.
  6. Garnish with fresh cilantro and serve with naan or rice.
